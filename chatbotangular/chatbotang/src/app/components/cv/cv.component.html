Top 5 mistakes to avoid on your CV



Your CV should make you stand out from the other candidates. But it's best to avoid doing so in a negative way! For this reason, we have compiled the ten main mistakes not to make on your resume

1. Do not put a title

"It is impossible to ignore a title," insists Michel Tosi, creator of a human resources consulting agency. "It works as a summary of your life or your professional aspiration and situates your level of responsibility. " ðŸ‘‰ Giving a title to your CV allows you to define a clear axis, and to show that your application is justified, and is not made at random.

2. Have a generic CV

It is essential to adapt your CV to the position for which you are applying. Obviously, this does not mean completely redoing your CV, or changing the entire layout. But it is important to adjust it a little, by changing the title, and by highlighting certain experiences or skills that are more in line with the offer you are responding to. "The recruiter must perceive that your CV is only intended for him for the position concerned", underlines Michel Tosi.

3. Mention all your experiences

This is the logical continuation of the previous point, but it is useless to put experiences that have nothing to do with the environment in which you work, just to fill your CV, it would be a big mistake.

4. Lie

Your CV is made to highlight you. So everyone tends to give themselves a bit of an advantage: "Recruiters are not fooled and they consider that 75% of CVs are 'optimized'," says Michel Tosi. But be careful not to fall into the lie.

5. Make a long CV

When you are at the beginning of your career, a classic mistake is to try to compensate by spreading out your experiences. It is logical that your CV should grow with time, but when you are leaving school, for example, your CV should fit on one page. If you already have several years of career, on the other hand, do not be too succinct. "From the age of 35, a one-page CV seems too short," explains Mireille Garolla.
