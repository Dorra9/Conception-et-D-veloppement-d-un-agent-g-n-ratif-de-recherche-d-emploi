How to highlight your language level in a CV?



In many professional environments, foreign languages have become essential skills. But in order to seduce recruiters, you need to be able to clearly express your level. For this, follow our advice!

1. Know how to evaluate your level

The first thing to do when talking about your language level on your resume is to know where you really stand. It is important to use clear and universal terms and references. For example, writing "English read, written and spoken" does not indicate your level at all. Also, avoid vague formulas such as "notions" or "school level", which in reality indicate a fairly low level. When making your CV, always think about the fact that some skills you don't know much about may not be worth mentioning, as they will do you more harm than good. First of all, use the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages, which you probably heard about in school. It is a six-level scale that measures foreign language learning in Europe.

Soft skills, or behavioral skills, are just as important, and sometimes even more so. 2.Define and illustrate your level

Once you have determined what skills you want to highlight on your resume, it is important to know how to do so. Listing skills without detailing them will not be very useful: it is too vague and does not really indicate to the recruiter your level in these areas. To avoid this, consider trying to quantify your skills as much as possible, especially technical ones. üîî You can do this through exams, or any other type of certification that you have obtained. For example, this is what it is advisable to do for language levels. If this is not possible, feel free to give specifics to describe your level. For example, if you are an expert in Photoshop, give examples of what you can do with it. If they are less tangible skills, such as management, it is even more crucial to make it concrete: you can specify "management of a team of 10 people on graphic design projects" for example. For soft skills, which by definition are abstract, you can describe your mastery by mentioning the experiences where you had to use these skills. If you have worked a lot independently in a communications position, for example, you can mention this to highlight your ability to take initiative and organize yourself. The idea is to try to illustrate these human skills in your background.
